More film industry jobs that pay well

Producer:

A film producer works under an executive producer but above a director to oversee the
production of a film and ensure it follows the plans and budgets they established before filming.
They perform work like selecting and editing scripts, casting actors in various
roles and managing business aspects of filming, like contracts and funding.

Screenwriter:

A screenwriter writes the script or screenplay for a film either as an
independent project, which they can pitch to executives to turn
into a movie, or after they're hired to complete a specific film project.
To do this, they may collaborate with executives and complete research
to write a screenplay that includes dialogue, stage directions and setting details.

Cinematographer:

A cinematographer decides and executes the creative direction, or cinematography,
of a film production. This may include directing the videographer along with the film,
light and sound crews to create a visual style for a film that appeals to
audiences and gives the story being told a specific aesthetic, mood and tone.

Talent agent:

A talent agent works with talent in the film industry, like actors and musicians who audition
for roles or have their music featured on movie soundtracks. Talent agents may scout actors and
musicians or meet them at an agency, after which agents find auditions that fit their client's
style and preferences and contact casting directors or soundtrack composers for meetings.

Actor:

An actor is a drama and performance professional who acts out roles as various
characters in movies or television. These professionals research their roles,
read scripts and make suggestions and practice during rehearsals to understand the characters
they need to portray and create an emotional response in audience members with their acting.

Video editor:

A video editor takes the footage filmed during production and cuts,
rearranges and adds transitions to it to put together a cohesive
storyline for a film. Editors use video editing software and work closely with directors,
cinematographers and screenwriters to make a movie that audience members can understand and enjoy.

Composer:

A composer is a music expert who writes music and lyrics
for musicians to perform and may help choose the songs for a movie soundtrack.
They may collaborate with the executives of a production to learn more
about the tone of a film and pick their music and instruments based on this information.
